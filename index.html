Computational target triage · ALS

Twenty ALS genes, one live evidence graph

This pipeline takes the 20-gene amyotrophic lateral sclerosis panel and pulls the evidence around the panel genes from six public databases: protein records from UniProt, interaction partners from STRING, classified variants from ClinVar, pathway membership from Reactome, literature from PubMed, and disease associations and known drugs from Open Targets. It links the records into one graph, scores the genes, and writes 309 candidate mechanisms tied to real citations. Rows trace back to cached API responses.

What this is

A reproducible evidence graph, not a hand-built dataset

The 20 seed genes resolve to UniProt accessions, then query across five more sources. The responses are cached on disk and normalized into a small database: genes, interactions, variants, pathways, disease associations, drugs, and papers. The graph that results carries 190 interaction edges, 1,479 variants, 102 Reactome pathways, and 165 papers with 110 distinct PubMed ids.

Wiping the cache and running again rebuilds the database to the same shape, which is the test that no row was seeded by hand. The full provenance, with spot-checked records and their source URLs, ships with the project.

Generated hypotheses

The 309 candidate mechanisms, searchable

The pipeline writes a hypothesis for the shared-pathway, network-proximity, and variant-convergence motifs it finds in the graph. The hypotheses name the genes involved and cite real PubMed papers that mention them. Search the text, or filter by mechanism type and confidence. The full set is here and ready to browse.

Notes & provenance

Data comes from UniProt, STRING, NCBI ClinVar and PubMed, Reactome, and Open Targets. The public APIs are queried live and cached for reproducibility. Variant classifications use ClinVar germline classifications; drug stages come from Open Targets. This page is a worked example of an evidence-integration pipeline. It is informational and is not medical advice.
